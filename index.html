Mass Spectrometry, Reimagined for Larger Ideas.

Our charge detection mass spectrometer (CDMS) is a new tool that helps research laboratories who need to rapidly quantify samples containing MDa+ molecules, such as polymers, viruses, DNA, and protein complexes. Unlike other techniques, our proprietary technology allows us to measure the mass of these analytes in minutes (not hours) and only minimal sample preparation is required.

How We Can Help You

We provide a simple, fast way to determine the fidelity of your virus assembly, determine the mass distribution of your polymer preparation, or explore any other MDa-sized questions.

Learn More

Problem

There are no commercial methods to reliably measure the mass of megadalton sized molecules, and other techniques that probe this size range require painstaking preparation and significant investment.

Learn More

Solution

Charge detection mass spectrometry measures both the charge and the mass-to-charge ratio of individual molecules, circumventing the challenges associated with weighing MDa+ molecules that bog down conventional mass spectrometry techniques.

Benefits

Our charge detection mass spectromter:

  • can operate on uncleaned samples
  • can measure megadalton+ sized molecules
  • outputs data that is can be adapted to whatever environment you use
  • is cheaper that competing techniques, such as dynamic light scattering, conventional mass spectrometry, and tunneling electron microscopy

Using technology invented right here at UC Berkeley, we have designed a charge detection mass spectrometer that can measure the masses of these large molecules in their native, whole state. It works by measuring both the charge and mass to charge ratio of individual molecules, instead of merely measuring the mass to charge ratio—as is the case for conventional mass spectrometry techniques.
